# Plan Design Review Principles

Use this reference when `/plan-design-review` needs design judgment beyond the active workflow.

## Core Principles

1. Empty states are features. Specify warmth, context, and a primary action.
2. Every screen needs explicit hierarchy: first, second, third.
3. Specificity beats vibes. Name typography, spacing, layout, and interaction patterns.
4. Edge cases are user experiences: long names, zero results, errors, first-time and power use.
5. AI slop is the enemy: generic cards, centered heroes, uniform grids, and default gradients fail.
6. Responsive does not mean "stacked on mobile"; each viewport needs intentional layout.
7. Accessibility is not optional: keyboard, screen reader, contrast, and touch targets must be specified.
8. Subtraction is the default. Every element must earn its pixels.
9. Trust is earned at the pixel level.

## Cognitive Patterns

- Seeing the system, not the screen: evaluate before, during, after, and failure states.
- Empathy as simulation: bad signal, one hand free, boss watching, first time versus thousandth time.
- Hierarchy as service: respect user attention.
- Constraint worship: if only three things fit, choose the three that matter.
- Question reflex: ask who it is for and what they tried before.
- Edge-case paranoia: long text, zero data, network failures, color blindness, RTL.
- Would-I-notice test: invisible support is often the best design.
- Principled taste: "feels wrong" must map to a broken principle.
- Time-horizon design: 5 seconds visceral, 5 minutes behavioral, 5 years reflective.
- Storyboard the journey: every moment has a mood, not just a layout.

References: Dieter Rams' 10 principles, Don Norman's three levels of design, Nielsen heuristics, Gestalt principles, Ira Glass on taste, Jony Ive on care, and Joe Gebbia on trust and storyboarding.
