## Goal

Raise `/plan` SKILL.md prose discipline from 1.2/5 → 4.0+/5 by adding 3 Iron Laws that codify (a) per-task acceptance criteria, (b) hypothesis-driven planning, (c) plan→build handoff binding.

After this lands:
- Plans can no longer ship as vague TODO lists. Every task in `sprint-contract.md` is observable + verifiable.
- Plans articulate what could fail before `/build` starts. No silent risks.
- `/build` cannot expand scope mid-execution. Scope creep is a routing event, not a unilateral decision.

---

## Why this is largest single ROI

Per audit: **`/plan` 1.2/5** is the lowest-discipline canonical skill. Plans are the contract `/build` executes against. Every downstream stage (`/build`, `/review`, `/qa`) inherits `/plan`'s ambiguity. Strengthening `/plan` is the single highest-leverage prose change Track E can make.

Compare to Phase E.1 (`/build` 1.6/5 → strengthened): that work raised `/build`'s floor but didn't fix the upstream input. Strengthening `/plan` raises the input quality, which compounds into `/build`'s output quality independently.

GSD's `plan-phase` is the strongest upstream counterpart (per audit). This brief absorbs its **per-task verification loop** concept into Nexus prose without bundling GSD itself.

## Iron Law design

### Law 1 — Bite-Sized Task Contract

**Discipline absorbed from:** GSD `plan-phase` (per-task verification loop) + Superpowers `writing-plans` (content contract).

**Voice (Nexus-native):**

```markdown
### Law 1 — Bite-Sized Task Contract

Every task in `sprint-contract.md` MUST satisfy three properties:

1. **Bounded scope** — describable in ≤3 sentences; deliverable in ≤1 day of focused work. If a task takes longer than 1 day to even describe, it's not a task; it's a phase that needs further decomposition.
2. **Observable acceptance criterion** — written in the form "X exists at path Y AND Z is true" (or equivalent). The criterion must be verifiable WITHOUT running the implementation. Vague criteria like "code works" or "feature is good" are rejected.
3. **Verification command** — one specific command (`bun test path/to/test.ts`, `bun run repo:inventory:check`, etc.) that the operator runs to confirm the task is done. If the task has no verification command, it is too vague to plan.

A task that fails any of these three is rejected before `/plan` writes the contract. The cost of rejecting a vague task at `/plan` time is minutes; the cost of letting it through and discovering the ambiguity in `/build` is hours.
```

This Law operationalizes "bite-sized" with three concrete checks.

### Law 2 — Hypothesis Before Plan

**Discipline absorbed from:** PM `prd-development` (hypothesis-driven framing) + Superpowers `writing-plans` (risk articulation).

**Voice (Nexus-native):**

```markdown
### Law 2 — Hypothesis Before Plan

`sprint-contract.md` MUST contain a `## Risks` section listing ≥3 specific failure modes the plan anticipates, each with:

- **Failure description** — what could go wrong, in concrete terms
- **Detection signal** — how the operator would notice this happening (output of which command, which artifact field)
- **Mitigation** — what the plan does pre-emptively, OR what the recovery routing is (e.g., "back to `/discover`", "escalate via AskUserQuestion")

Plans without an explicit risk section are rejected. "I don't see any risks" is itself a risk; if you cannot enumerate ≥3, the plan is under-thought.

Common failure modes worth listing (not exhaustive):
- Underestimated scope of a task (task that looks bite-sized but isn't)
- Missing dependency the plan assumes is installed/available
- Acceptance criterion that's measurable but doesn't capture user value
- Cross-task coupling that wasn't modeled

The risk section is read by `/build` as predecessor context. When a risk materializes during `/build`, the recovery routing is already known — no scrambling.
```

This Law forces explicit anticipation, not optimistic planning.

### Law 3 — Plan→Build Handoff Is Binding

**Discipline absorbed from:** GSD `pause-work`/`resume-work` (handoff contract) + Superpowers `executing-plans` (don't drift from plan).

**Voice (Nexus-native):**

```markdown
### Law 3 — Plan→Build Handoff Is Binding

Once `/plan` writes `sprint-contract.md` AND `/handoff` records the route, `/build` operates ONLY on the tasks listed in the contract. Scope expansion mid-`/build` is forbidden:

- If `/build` discovers an additional task is needed → DO NOT add it silently. Stop, route back to `/plan` to extend the contract, then re-enter `/build`.
- If `/build` discovers a listed task is wrong/impossible → DO NOT skip it silently. Stop, route back to `/plan` with the discovered constraint, then re-enter `/build`.
- If `/build` finishes faster than expected → leave remaining capacity unused; do not pull in adjacent work that wasn't contracted.

The reason: the plan is the contract for review and quality. `/review` and `/qa` evaluate `/build` against the plan. Silent scope expansion makes `/review` impossible — there's no agreed acceptance criterion for the expanded work.

Scope creep is a routing event, not a unilateral decision.
```

This Law preserves the integrity of `/review` and `/qa` by binding `/build` to the contract.

## Voice constraints (CRITICAL)

This is the part that distinguishes Nexus's strengthened prose from a Superpowers wrapper. Per `/build` PoC voice:

| Pattern | Don't | Do |
|---|---|---|
| Verification framing | "Run failing test first then code" | "Empty output ≠ verified. The runtime advisor reads what you attached, not what you remember running." |
| Stop-and-ask trigger | "Three strikes you're out" | "Patching past 3 strikes without confirmed root cause is the most common path to a wrong fix shipped under stage-status `ready`." |
| Cross-skill flow | "Hand off to debugging" | "Route to `/investigate` — its 4-phase protocol (locate → analyze → hypothesize → fix) is purpose-built for stuckness." |
| Mandatory rule prefix | "Iron Law:" alone | "These three rules apply to every `/build` invocation regardless of provider, topology, or run mode. They are short and absolute on purpose — discipline that lives in qualifiers does not survive contact with the LLM at decision time." |

For E.2: replace `/build`-specific phrasing with `/plan`-specific equivalents (sprint-contract.md, plan→build handoff, etc.). Keep the Nexus-voice meta-justification ("short and absolute on purpose") because it explains WHY hard rules.

## Risk register

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Iron Law 1's "≤1 day" is too rigid for some tasks | Acceptable — Law 1 is a heuristic; tasks longer than 1 day are decomposable into smaller tasks. If reviewers find this rule blocks legitimate work, soften in a follow-up. |
| Risk section feels formulaic ("we have to write 3 risks even if we have 2") | Voice notes that "I don't see any risks" is itself a risk. The third risk can always be "scope underestimation" if nothing else applies. |
| Scope-binding Law 3 makes `/build` rigid in fast-iteration scenarios | Bouncing back to `/plan` is fast (minutes); the cost is alignment, not throughput. Faster than discovering scope creep at `/review`. |
| `/plan` users don't read the Iron Laws because the section is long | Section is ~75 lines; comparable to `/build` PoC. Each Law is ≤25 lines. Skim-friendly with bold headers. |
| Voice feels "preachy" or "academic" | Per voice guide: short paragraphs, end with what to do, no AI vocabulary. Brief reviewer should flag any preachy sentence. |
