## Goal

Raise `/review` SKILL.md prose discipline from 2.0/5 → 4.0+/5 by adding 3 Iron Laws that codify (a) what every review must produce, (b) when single-pass is insufficient and a second round is mandatory, (c) how to handle disagreement between audit slots without silently averaging.

After this lands:
- A `/review` invocation cannot return a verdict without four mandatory artifacts visible in the advisor record (scope, test evidence, risks, verdict).
- Block-grade advisories cannot be closed without a verifying second pass.
- Conflicting audit slots (codex vs gemini vs local) cannot be silently averaged; disagreement is surfaced explicitly.

---

## Why this is rank 3 ROI

`/review` is the gate `/ship` reads. Today its prose is governance language ("dual-audit completion," "audit provenance consistency") with no engineering review discipline. The runtime stage pack already declares `absorbed_capability: 'superpowers-review-discipline'` — but that absorption is invisible at decision time because the prose never re-asserts it. Per audit:

> "There's no 'before claiming the gate is open, what do you require to see,' no taxonomy of review smells, and no rule for handling reviewer disagreement."

Strengthening `/review` raises the bar between `/build` and `/ship` — the single highest-leverage place to catch regressions before they merge.

Compare to E.2 (`/plan`): plan strengthening fixes input quality. Review strengthening fixes the gate. Together they bracket `/build`.

## Iron Law design

### Law 1 — Review Content Contract

**Discipline absorbed from:** Superpowers `requesting-code-review` + `verification-before-completion` + audit recommendation #1.

**Voice (Nexus-native):**

```markdown
### Law 1 — Review Content Contract

A `/review` verdict — `pass`, `pass_with_advisories`, or `fail` — requires four artifacts visible in the advisor record at decision time:

1. **Scope summary** — what changed, in concrete terms (files touched, behavior modified). Not "the PR diff" as a substitute; a one-paragraph synthesis the reviewer wrote in this turn.
2. **Fresh test evidence** — the verification command for this change ran in the current message AND its output is attached. Stale CI runs from a previous turn are not evidence — they describe a different state of the tree.
3. **Risk register** — listed risks with severity (`block`, `advisory`, `nit`). An empty list is acceptable only when paired with a one-sentence justification. "Looks good" without enumeration is not a risk register.
4. **Explicit gate verdict** — one of `pass` | `pass_with_advisories` | `fail`, written into the advisor record in this turn. The runtime advisor reads what is attached, not what is remembered.

A review that omits any of the four is not a review; it is an opinion. Opinions don't open gates.
```

This Law operationalizes "before claiming the gate is open, what do you require to see."

### Law 2 — Two-Round Protocol For Block-Grade Advisories

**Discipline absorbed from:** Superpowers `receiving-code-review` (address-and-verify loop) + RFC sketch #1.

**Voice (Nexus-native):**

```markdown
### Law 2 — Two-Round Protocol For Block-Grade Advisories

When Pass 1 of `/review` produces ANY `block`-severity advisory, single-pass completion is forbidden:

1. The block-grade advisory is recorded in the advisor record.
2. `/build` is re-entered to address it (per `/build` Law 3 — prior advisories are address-or-dispute).
3. `/review` is re-entered for Pass 2. Pass 2 verifies the specific advisory has been closed AND no regression was introduced. Re-running the same audit set is the minimum; spot-checking the changed area is the recommended.
4. Only after Pass 2 completes clean may the gate verdict become `pass` or `pass_with_advisories`.

Pass 1 alone never produces `pass` when block-grade advisories exist. The advisor record carries the round number (`pass_round: 1` or `2`) and downstream `/ship` reads it.

Single-round review on block-grade findings is the most common path to "we shipped a regression that the review caught."
```

This Law makes "verify advisory closed" a structural step, not a discretionary one.

### Law 3 — Reviewer Disagreement Protocol

**Discipline absorbed from:** Superpowers `receiving-code-review` (don't average outside voices) + audit recommendation #3.

**Voice (Nexus-native):**

```markdown
### Law 3 — Reviewer Disagreement Protocol

When 2+ audit slots disagree on a finding (e.g., `codex` flags `block`, `gemini` flags `nit`, `local_a` is silent), the synthesis MUST enumerate the disagreement explicitly. Silent averaging is forbidden.

For every disagreed finding, the synthesis records:

- **The conflicting verdicts** — which slot said what
- **The accepted verdict** — which slot's call the synthesis is taking
- **The rationale** — why this slot is being trusted on this specific finding (e.g., "codex inspected the failure path the others didn't read"; "gemini misread the type signature")
- **The pushed-back verdicts** — which slot calls are being explicitly NOT followed, and why

When synthesis cannot choose without speculation, escalate via `AskUserQuestion`. Do not pick the majority and move on; do not pick the most cautious and move on. Either choice without rationale is silent averaging.

The reason: outside voices are signals, not votes. Two-out-of-three agreement on a wrong call is still a wrong call. The synthesis is responsible for the verdict, and the advisor record carries that responsibility.
```

This Law preserves the integrity of dual-audit by making disagreement a first-class artifact, not noise to be smoothed over.

## Voice constraints (CRITICAL)

This is the part that distinguishes Nexus's strengthened prose from a Superpowers wrapper. Per E.1/E.2 PoC voice:

| Pattern | Don't | Do |
|---|---|---|
| Verification framing | "Run failing test first" | "The runtime advisor reads what is attached, not what is remembered." |
| Stop-and-ask trigger | "Two-round review" alone | "Pass 1 alone never produces `pass` when block-grade advisories exist. … Single-round review on block-grade findings is the most common path to 'we shipped a regression that the review caught.'" |

Reuse the meta-justification ("short and absolute on purpose") from E.1/E.2 — it explains WHY hard rules exist and is voice-consistent across all Track E phases.

## Cross-Iron-Law coherence

Track E builds a coherent voice. After E.3, the cross-skill rule chain reads:

- **`/plan` Law 3** (Plan→Build Handoff Is Binding) → tasks bound to contract
- **`/build` Law 3** (Prior Advisories Are Address-Or-Dispute) → reviewer findings can't be silently dropped
- **`/review` Law 2** (Two-Round Protocol) → block-grade advisories trigger re-entry into /build, then /review verifies closure
- **`/review` Law 3** (Reviewer Disagreement Protocol) → synthesis enumerates conflicts; AskUserQuestion when synthesis can't choose

This forms a closed loop: `/plan` constrains scope → `/build` executes → `/review` enforces evidence + handles disagreement → loop back to `/build` if needed → re-verify.

The chain is what makes the strengthened prose more than per-skill polish. It is governance discipline at the prose layer where the LLM reads it.

## Risk register

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Law 2's "two-round" feels heavy when Pass 1 produces only nits | Law 2 explicitly scopes to `block`-grade advisories. `pass_with_advisories` for nits-only does NOT trigger Pass 2. |
| Law 3's "AskUserQuestion when synthesis can't choose" creates user-prompt friction | Acceptable. The friction is the point — synthesizer-without-rationale is silent averaging. If this rule fires too often, the audit prompts need strengthening, not the rule. |
| "Fresh test evidence" requirement creates re-run cost when CI already ran | Necessary. Stale CI from a previous turn describes a different tree. The rule mirrors `/build` Law 1 ("evidence before claims") for voice consistency. |
| Three-slot disagreement is rare in practice; rule looks academic | Even rare disagreement matters when it happens. The rule encodes the protocol so when it fires, the response is structured. |
| Iron Laws section lengthens SKILL.md to ~570+ lines | Same scale as `/build` (565 lines) and `/plan` (567 lines) post-strengthening. Within design budget. |
