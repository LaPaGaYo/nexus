## Goal

Raise `/discover` SKILL.md prose discipline from 1.4/5 → 4.0+/5 by adding 3 Iron Laws that codify (a) what `/discover` is NOT (the anti-patterns it must reject), (b) what minimum signal `idea-brief.md` must carry before advancing to `/frame`, (c) the discipline of separating team assumptions from gathered evidence.

After this lands:
- A `/discover` invocation cannot drift into premature solutioning, single-stakeholder framing, or "discovery from vibes."
- `idea-brief.md` cannot reach `/frame` without ≥1 named user segment, ≥1 observed pain with cost, ≥2 evidence sources, and explicit open questions.
- The artifact distinguishes "what we assumed" from "what we observed" — bias-resistant framing pre-empts the trap of confirmation-only discovery.

## Why this is rank 6 ROI

`/discover` is the entry point. Today its prose is 3 bullets — "capture goals, capture constraints, record open questions" — and the SKILL.md doesn't tell the model whether to ask questions, when to stop, or what makes a discovery "ready" for `/frame`.

Per audit:

> "PM `discovery-process` runs 6 phases over 2-4 weeks with explicit anti-patterns ('not waterfall research,' 'not user testing'), evidence requirements (interviews, synthesis, experiments), and decision gates (build/pivot/kill). Nexus discover doesn't even tell the model whether to ask questions, when to stop, or what makes a discovery 'ready' for `/frame`."

E.5 strengthened `/frame`'s output discipline (PRD content contract, hypothesis framing, readiness gate). E.6 strengthens `/frame`'s input discipline. The two together raise the floor of everything downstream — `/plan` reads framing that came from disciplined discovery rather than from vibes.

This phase also closes the entry-edge of the Iron Law chain. After E.6, every stage from entry to QA carries Iron Laws.

## Iron Law design

### Law 1 — What `/discover` Is NOT

**Discipline absorbed from:** PM `discovery-process` anti-patterns + audit recommendation #1.

**Voice (Nexus-native):**

```markdown
### Law 1 — What `/discover` Is NOT

`/discover` rejects these patterns at entry. If the work is drifting into any of them, stop and reframe:

1. **Not a feature spec** — discovery does not produce "build X with Y fields and Z button." That is `/plan`'s output. Discovery output is a problem statement; the solution comes after `/frame` confirms scope.
2. **Not user testing** — discovery is not "have users click on prototypes and tell us what they think." It is upstream of any artifact to test. If the work is testing something, it is not yet discovery.
3. **Not a substitute for shipping** — discovery does not replace learning from a shipped change. If a small slice can be shipped to validate the hypothesis, route to `pol-probe` (lightweight validation) instead of expanding discovery scope.
4. **Not single-stakeholder framing** — discovery from one person's view of the problem is biased framing. The artifact must reflect ≥2 perspectives (user + owner, owner + ops, etc.); single-source discovery is captured but flagged as a known limitation.
5. **Not solutioning in disguise** — "we should add X" is a solution. "Users currently fail at Y because Z" is discovery. If the brief reads like a feature list, discovery hasn't started yet.

A `/discover` artifact that reads like any of these is a category error. Re-enter discovery rather than passing the artifact to `/frame`.
```

This Law makes the rejection criteria explicit so drift is caught at discovery time, not at framing time.

### Law 2 — Minimum Signal Bar Before `/frame`

**Discipline absorbed from:** PM `discovery-process` evidence requirements + audit recommendation #2 + RFC sketches #2 and #3.

**Voice (Nexus-native):**

```markdown
### Law 2 — Minimum Signal Bar Before `/frame`

`docs/product/idea-brief.md` MUST carry the following before `/discover` writes `status.json` with verdict `ready`:

1. **Named user segment** — not "users." A specific segment with a name (e.g., "operators running multi-provider Nexus on macOS"). The name does not have to be a customer; it has to be specific enough that ≠ "everyone."
2. **Observed pain with cost** — what the named user fails at and what that failure costs (time, money, churn, broken trust). "Users are frustrated" fails; "operators run /build, hit a 3-strike loop, and abandon the session 18% of the time per session-archive logs" passes.
3. **≥2 evidence sources** — at least two distinct sources informed this discovery. Sources count as: user interviews, support tickets, metrics, prior PRDs, market signals, internal usage logs, customer email threads. Two sources of the same type (e.g., two interview transcripts) count as two; same source quoted twice does not.
4. **Hypothesis hint** — a one-paragraph version of the "If/Then/Because" hypothesis (per `/frame` Law 2) that `/frame` will sharpen. The hint may be incomplete; the framing pass refines it. But discovery without a hypothesis hint is observation, not direction.
5. **Open questions list** — explicit questions for `/frame` to answer, ≥3 items. "What's the success metric?", "Which segment ships first?", "What's the rollback plan?" are typical. Empty open-questions sections mean discovery overclaimed.

If any of the five fail, `/discover` returns `not_ready`. `/frame` reads the verdict from the advisor record. If `not_ready`, `/frame` cannot proceed.

Discovery-from-vibes is a real failure mode. The signal bar exists because two interviews + one customer email is cheaper than three weeks of `/build` based on guessed framing.
```

This Law gives `/frame` something concrete to receive — and gives the operator a stop-bar for "is discovery enough?"

### Law 3 — Look Inward Before Look Outward

**Discipline absorbed from:** PM `problem-framing-canvas` (Look Inward / Look Outward / Reframe) + audit recommendation #4.

**Voice (Nexus-native):**

```markdown
### Law 3 — Look Inward Before Look Outward

`idea-brief.md` MUST contain two clearly separated sections, in this order:

1. **Look Inward** — what the team currently believes, before gathering evidence. Captures team assumptions, prior framings, the hypothesis the team is bringing in. This section is written FIRST so the team's prior is on the record.
2. **Look Outward** — what evidence was gathered (interviews, tickets, metrics, etc.) and what it actually said. Cite each piece of evidence to its source per Law 2.

Without **Look Inward**, the team cannot tell what its prior was — and cannot tell whether its evidence collection was confirming or challenging that prior. That is the trap of confirmation-only discovery: the team interviews until interviews agree with what the team already thought.

The brief MAY end with a brief **Reframe** section noting where Look Outward changed Look Inward — what the team thought going in vs. what it concluded. The Reframe is the receipt that discovery happened.

The reason: a discovery brief that does not record the team's prior is unfalsifiable as a discovery output. There is no way to tell whether the brief reflects what users said or what the team wanted users to say.
```

This Law makes confirmation bias visible at the artifact layer.

## Cross-Iron-Law coherence

After E.6, the Iron Law chain spans the full lifecycle from problem-recognition through QA verdict:

- **`/discover` Law 1** (anti-patterns: not-feature-spec, not-user-testing, not-substitute-for-shipping, not-single-stakeholder, not-solutioning)
- **`/discover` Law 2** (minimum signal: named segment, observed pain w/ cost, ≥2 evidence sources, hypothesis hint, ≥3 open questions) → feeds `/frame` input
- **`/discover` Law 3** (Look Inward before Look Outward) → confirmation bias visible at artifact layer
- **`/frame` Law 1** (PRD 7-section contract)
- **`/frame` Law 2** (hypothesis framing — sharpens `/discover` Law 2 hypothesis hint)
- **`/frame` Law 3** (readiness gate before `/plan`)
- ... continues through `/plan`, `/build`, `/review`, `/qa`

The handshake at the `/discover` → `/frame` boundary is now explicit:

- `/discover` Law 2 produces a *hypothesis hint*
- `/frame` Law 2 sharpens it into the formal "If we / then / because" hypothesis
- `/frame` Law 3 verifies all four hypothesis clauses are populated before `/plan`

This is the closing of the input edge. From here, no stage in the lifecycle lacks Iron Laws.

## Risk register

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Law 1's 5-item "is NOT" list is the longest enumeration in any Iron Law so far | Acceptable. Anti-pattern lists are categorically different from positive rule lists; 5 short items reads faster than 3 long items. The audit explicitly recommended 4-5. |
| Law 2's "≥2 evidence sources" can be gamed (two flimsy sources) | Voice-bake quality with examples. "Two interview transcripts" counts; "one customer email re-quoted twice" doesn't. The rule is a floor, not a ceiling. |
| Law 3's "Look Inward / Look Outward / Reframe" is jargon | The format is explicit on purpose — it's the recognizable industry pattern from `problem-framing-canvas`. New operators learn it once. The Reframe section is optional (MAY, not MUST) to avoid template overhead for short discoveries. |
| `/discover` doesn't currently include `AskUserQuestion`; adding it is a frontmatter change | Low risk. `/frame` and `/plan` both have it; the bias-resistant interview pattern Law 3 implies needs it. Symmetric with neighbors. |
| Iron Laws section + frontmatter change creates a slightly larger diff than prior phases | Acceptable. Frontmatter change is a single line. Total diff still within budget. |

1. **Law count** — 3 (matches E.1–E.5 cadence; audit identified 4 candidate gaps; merged "PoL probe trigger" into Law 1's "not substitute for shipping" anti-pattern)
2. **Anti-pattern count in Law 1** — 5 (audit recommended 4–5; chose 5 for full coverage of common drift patterns)
3. **Evidence source count in Law 2** — ≥2 (industry-typical floor for "more than vibes")

5. **Cross-skill linkage** — Law 1 references `pol-probe`; Law 2 references `/frame` Law 2 explicitly. Coherence checked.
6. **Look Inward / Look Outward / Reframe** — Look Inward + Look Outward MUST; Reframe MAY. Avoids template overhead for short discoveries while preserving the bias-resistance principle.
7. **Schema vs prose** — prose only. Schema work deferred.
